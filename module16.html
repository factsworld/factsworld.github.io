🛡️ Module 16: How to Protect Yourself from Wi-Fi Attacks

You’ve learned the dark arts, young padawan. Now it’s time to train as a cyber-defender. Here's how to make yourself (and your grandma) a tough nut to crack!

🔐 1. Use Strong WPA2/WPA3 Passwords

Never use 12345678 or password123. A good Wi-Fi password is like a burrito — it should be long, stuffed with randomness, and hard to unwrap.

🚫 2. Disable WPS

WPS is like leaving your house key under the doormat... labeled "house key." Disable it in your router settings — ASAP.

🕵️‍♂️ 3. Hide Your SSID

Broadcasting your SSID is like yelling your name in a crowd of hackers. You can hide it or use decoy names like FBI_SURVEILLANCE_VAN.

🔁 4. Regularly Update Router Firmware

Yes, even routers need glow-ups. Updates fix vulnerabilities. Check your router’s admin panel monthly.

🧯 5. Monitor Connected Devices

Use your router dashboard or tools like Fing to sniff out freeloaders. If someone’s watching Netflix on your bandwidth — terminate with extreme prejudice (or change your password).

🎭 6. Use MAC Address Filtering

Set your router to allow only known MAC addresses. Not foolproof, but it’s like checking IDs at a club. Spoofers might still sneak in — but it slows 'em down.

🌍 7. Don’t Auto-Connect to Open Networks

Public Wi-Fi is hacker hunting ground. Turn off auto-connect, or better yet, use a VPN — a digital invisibility cloak!

📵 8. Turn Off Wi-Fi When Not in Use

No Wi-Fi, no problem. No signal = no sniffing, spoofing, or hijacking. Especially when sleeping or not home.

💡 Pro Tip: Set up a guest network for friends & smart devices — with separate password and no LAN access.

📈 Final Thoughts

Now you’re not just a Wi-Fi hacker, but also a protector of the signal realm. Spread the word, patch those holes, and keep your packets private!
