👻 Module 10: Fake Access Points & Evil Twin Attacks

Welcome to the honey trap zone! In this module, we’ll build our own fake Wi-Fi networks and lure unsuspecting victims. Think of it like building a haunted house with free internet and watching people walk in. Spooky, right? 🎃

🔍 What is an Evil Twin Attack?

An evil twin is a malicious Wi-Fi access point that looks like a legitimate one. When a device connects, you can capture data, hijack sessions, or even redirect them to fake login pages. Trick or treat, hacker-style. 🍬

🧠 Real World Example

Imagine you're in an airport. You see "FreeAirportWiFi" and connect. Little do you know, it’s actually our evil twin sitting two seats away with a hoodie and Kali Linux. 🧑‍💻✈️

🛠️ Tools We’ll Use

  • airbase-ng – To create a fake AP
  • dnsmasq – For DHCP and DNS
  • hostapd – Flexible tool for creating real-looking APs
  • ettercap / Bettercap – For juicy man-in-the-middle data munching

🏗️ Setup: Creating the Fake AP

Let’s get our evil twin up and running with airbase-ng:

airbase-ng -e "FreeAirportWiFi" -c 6 wlan0mon

This creates a fake AP named FreeAirportWiFi on channel 6. Hook line and sinker!

🧲 Real Demo Reminder

⚠️ Reminder: Students, try this in a safe environment like a lab. A Raspberry Pi and a spare phone can simulate a real-world trap. Or trick your smart toaster. We don’t judge. 😁

🛡️ Captive Portal Option

You can redirect victims to a fake login page using dnsmasq + apache2. Make it look like a Starbucks login and see who types what. ☕👀

🌐 Example - DNS Redirection

iptables -t nat -A PREROUTING -p tcp --dport 80 -j REDIRECT --to-port 8080

📹 Bonus Tip

Record user actions using tcpdump or Wireshark. No actual evil needed – just knowledge. 🎥
