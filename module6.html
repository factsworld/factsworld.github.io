🎭 Module 6: Evil Twin Attacks – Becoming the Impostor

Imagine creating a Wi-Fi network that looks exactly like the one people trust... but it’s yours. 😈 Welcome to the world of Evil Twin Attacks — where deception is the weapon.

🧠 What Is an Evil Twin Attack?

An Evil Twin is a rogue Wi-Fi access point set up to mimic a legitimate one. When users connect, they think they’re on the real deal, but in reality, they’re handing over their data to you — the evil barista. ☕

🛠 Tools of the Trade

  • airbase-ng – to create a fake AP
  • dnsmasq – for DHCP/DNS redirection
  • hostapd – more advanced fake AP with better control
  • wifi-phisher – automated evil twin + phishing framework

💡 Scenario: Bob Connects to Free Coffee Shop Wi-Fi

You name your evil AP COFFEE_FREEWIFI. Bob’s device auto-connects. You redirect him to a fake login page asking for the Wi-Fi password (or worse, his email). He types boblovesespresso. Boom. 🎯

⚙️ Using WiFi-Pumpkin3 or WiFi-Phisher (Recommended for Beginners)

These tools automate Evil Twin attacks and provide phishing templates.

sudo wifi-phisher

Select your wireless adapter and pick the attack type (e.g., captive portal phishing).

📱 Tip: Make sure your Wi-Fi adapter supports AP (Access Point) mode!

⚠️ Ethics & Warnings

  • Always use a separate device or test network
  • Never target real users without consent
  • This attack can be illegal in many countries. Use for education and research only.

🔍 What Can You Do Once They Connect?

  • Phishing for credentials
  • Traffic sniffing (e.g., unencrypted HTTP sites)
  • Inject malicious updates/pages

📦 Summary

  • Evil Twin = fake AP that looks legit
  • Used to trick users into connecting & giving up info
  • Tools like WiFi-Phisher make it beginner-friendly
  • Ethics and legality matter: be a hacker, not a criminal
